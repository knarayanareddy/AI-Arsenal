## Overview

Last Week in AI is a news digest and companion podcast summarizing the week's AI developments across four recurring sections: tools & apps, applications & business, research & advancements, and policy & safety -- with more editorial framing per item than a pure link digest.

## Who it's for

Practitioners and researchers who want a single weekly-ish synthesis that includes policy and safety coverage alongside technical news; the podcast format also suits commute-time listening.

## What you'll get

- A sectioned digest with short editorial summaries per story, not bare links
- A companion podcast episode discussing the same week's top stories with added commentary
- Consistent inclusion of policy/regulation and safety stories that engineering-focused digests often skip

## How to get value fast

Subscribe on Substack and jump to the sections that match your interests -- the fixed section structure makes partial reading efficient. If you prefer audio, the podcast covers the same material with more context.

## What to avoid

Don't rely on it as your sole current-awareness source: the cadence is not strictly weekly, and gaps of several weeks occur (a ~6-week gap was observable in the feed as of 2026-07-08). Pair with a daily feed like TLDR AI for timeliness.

## Activity & health

The publication's RSS feed shows the most recent issues dated 2026-05-27, 2026-05-26, and 2026-05-20 -- roughly six weeks before the check date (checked 2026-07-08). Activity comes in bursts with pauses, supporting `activity_level: intermittent` rather than active.

## Safety & moderation

No moderation or trust concerns -- a one-way editorial newsletter and podcast, not an interactive space.

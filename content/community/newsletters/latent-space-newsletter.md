## Overview

Latent Space's newsletter arm, built around a near-daily "AINews" digest (aggregating subreddit/Twitter/Discord AI discussion) plus weekly essays and podcast show notes, run by the same team behind the Latent Space podcast and the AI Engineer conference series.

## Who it's for

Practitioners and founders who want a high-frequency pulse on what the AI engineering community is discussing day-to-day, not just headline model releases. The multi-cadence subscription model (daily/weekly/essay) makes it adaptable to how much volume a reader actually wants.

## What you'll get

- A near-daily "AINews" digest summarizing cross-platform AI engineering discussion (subreddits, X/Twitter, some Discords)
- Weekly essays and podcast show notes from the same team running the Latent Space podcast and AI Engineer conference series
- Direct visibility into AI Engineer conference planning and speaker announcements, since the newsletter and event series share ownership

## How to get value fast

Subscribe and explicitly configure your preferred cadence -- Latent Space supports separately opting in/out of the daily AINews digest, the weekly podcast notes, and the broader essay emails. Choose the tier matching how much daily AI-news volume you actually want; defaulting to all three can be overwhelming.

## What to avoid

Don't default into all three publishing cadences if you want a lighter-touch signal -- the daily AINews volume is genuinely high (the author explicitly checks "12 subreddits, 544 Twitters" per issue as described in the newsletter's own recurring disclaimer), and most readers are better served picking one or two tiers.

## Activity & health

Confirmed near-daily AINews publishing cadence plus weekly essays via a dated RSS feed showing entries through 2026-06-14, and a first-party January 2026 post by the author ("Scaling without Slop") citing 80,000 AINews subscribers and explicitly describing the daily/weekly/broader-essay publishing structure that remains current as of this check (checked 2026-07-06).

## Safety & moderation

No moderation or trust concerns apply -- this is primarily a one-way editorial newsletter, though it does support reader comments via Substack.

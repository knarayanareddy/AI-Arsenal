## Overview

LangChain's official Discourse-based help forum, separate from the real-time Slack, covering OSS product help (LangChain, LangGraph, Deep Agents), LangSmith product help (Observability & Evals, Deployment, Fleet), and LangChain Academy course discussion.

## Who it's for

Developers who prefer searchable, asynchronous Q&A over real-time chat, or who are debugging an issue likely already answered in a prior thread. Also useful for LangSmith (observability/eval tooling) questions specifically, which have their own dedicated forum category distinct from general LangChain/LangGraph help.

## What you'll get

- Indexed, searchable answers to common LangChain/LangGraph/LangSmith questions
- Direct-from-LangChain-team announcements in the Announcements category
- A lower-noise, asynchronous alternative to the Slack for non-urgent questions

## How to get value fast

Search before posting -- unlike the Slack, this forum is indexed and a large fraction of common questions already have answers. Post in the correct sub-category: `LangGraph`, `LangChain`, or `Deep Agents` under OSS Product Help, or `Observability & Evals`, `Deployment`, or `Fleet` under LangSmith Product Help, rather than the catch-all Talking Shop category.

## What to avoid

Don't expect real-time responses here -- the busiest category (OSS Product Help) shows roughly 30 new topics per month as of this review, meaningfully slower-paced than the Slack. If you need a fast answer, use [LangChain Community Slack](../chat/langchain-slack.md) instead.

## Activity & health

A direct fetch of the forum's category page on 2026-07-06 shows live per-category topic-per-month counts: Talking Shop 16/month, OSS Product Help 30/month, LangSmith Observability & Evals 10/month, LangChain Academy 2/month. This is first-party, directly observed data (not a third-party estimate), supporting `activity_level: active`.

## Overview

DeepLearning.AI's YouTube channel, hosting free and paid short-course video content on practical AI engineering topics (RAG, agents, evaluation, prompt engineering, ML foundations).

## Who it's for

Beginners and practitioners who prefer structured, course-format video content over a news/commentary channel format. Complements DeepLearning.AI's written newsletter, The Batch, for a different consumption style.

## What you'll get

- Structured, course-format video content covering practical AI engineering skills, often produced in partnership with named practitioners and companies
- Free short courses covering current tools and techniques (RAG pipelines, agent frameworks, evaluation methods) as they become relevant
- A large existing back catalog suitable for structured, sequential learning rather than one-off video consumption

## How to get value fast

Treat this channel as a structured course library rather than a news feed -- browse or search for a specific skill (e.g. "agents," "RAG," "evaluation") rather than expecting a chronological feed to surface what's relevant. Cross-reference with DeepLearning.AI's own course catalog, since new short courses are frequently announced there in tandem with the corresponding video content.

## What to avoid

Don't expect a fixed daily or weekly upload cadence -- unlike The Batch newsletter's confirmed weekly schedule, this channel's publishing follows an irregular course-release cycle.

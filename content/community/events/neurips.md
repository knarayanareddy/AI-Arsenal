## Overview

NeurIPS is one of the two or three most influential machine-learning research conferences: an annual, peer-reviewed venue whose proceedings, workshops, and competitions have introduced a large share of the foundational work behind modern LLMs (including the transformer architecture itself).

## Who it's for

Researchers publishing or tracking the field's frontier, and practitioners who want a once-a-year concentrated view of where research is heading -- attendance optional, since proceedings and most talks are freely available online.

## What you'll get

- The annual accepted-papers list -- one of the field's strongest curation signals for research quality
- Workshops that concentrate emerging subfields (efficient inference, agents, alignment) a year before they mainstream
- Public proceedings, posters, and recorded talks usable without attending

## How to get value fast

Treat the conference as a corpus, not only an event: when the accepted-papers list drops, filter by your area and read abstracts systematically. OpenReview hosts reviews and rebuttals for accepted work -- often as informative as the papers. If attending in person, spend time in poster sessions and workshops over main-track talks.

## What to avoid

Don't assume acceptance equals production relevance -- NeurIPS optimizes for research novelty, and many accepted methods never survive contact with production constraints. Filter through your own evals before adopting anything.

No material concerns: an established academic conference with a formal code of conduct and professional organization.

## Overview

The AI Engineer World's Fair is an annual, large-scale technical conference for AI engineers, run by the team behind the Latent Space newsletter and podcast, focused on practical AI engineering: agents, evaluation, infrastructure, and production deployment patterns.

## Who it's for

Practitioners, founders, and enterprise teams who want deep, current, engineering-first content on building with LLMs and agents -- distinct from more executive-/business-strategy-focused AI conferences. A companion smaller edition (AI Engineer Europe) runs in London for a similar but more regional audience.

## What you'll get

- A very large technical program: the 2026 main edition ran 29 tracks and 400+ sessions across multiple days
- A dedicated full workshop day (Day 1) separate from the main multi-track conference days
- Direct access to 100+ expo partners spanning foundation-model providers to GPU/infra vendors

## How to get value fast

Register early -- 2026 pricing sources describe a tiered structure ($999-$1,699 depending on timing and tier) with meaningful early-bird savings. Plan your track/session selections in advance given the scale (29 tracks, 400+ sessions) rather than trying to browse on-site. If attending the workshop day, treat it as a distinct, more hands-on track from the main conference.

## What to avoid

Don't treat this as a small, easily-browsable single-track event -- at this scale, arriving without a session plan means missing relevant talks that overlap in parallel tracks.

## Activity & health

The 2026 main edition is confirmed by multiple independently cross-checked event-listing sources to have run June 29 - July 2, 2026 in San Francisco, with a companion AI Engineer Europe 2026 edition having run April 8-10, 2026 in London (checked 2026-07-06).

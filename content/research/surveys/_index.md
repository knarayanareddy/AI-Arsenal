# Surveys Research

## What belongs here

Comprehensive literature surveys of a subfield, included only when the survey itself functions as a primary engineering reference — engineers use it as a navigational map of a subfield's landscape and tradeoffs, not merely as a reading list or academic review.

## What does NOT belong here

A survey that is comprehensive but mainly useful for academic literature-review purposes, rather than for making an actual engineering decision, does not meet the inclusion bar — "it is a comprehensive review" is not sufficient justification; "engineers use this to decide between approaches" is. Most technique-specific papers belong in one of the other 7 phase folders instead, even if they contain a substantial related-work section.

## Engineering frame

When I need a map of an entire subfield's landscape — not a single technique, but the tradeoffs across many competing approaches — is there a survey an engineer would actually reach for instead of reading a dozen individual papers?
